Pipe

Description

Following Polyanin2002 section 3.5 or Lehktmakher1971

Given steady laminar flow in a cylindrical pipe, with a stepwise increase in wall temperature at z=0, these functions calculate the temperature field at any point in the fluid after the increase (z>0).

\[ \textbf{Pe} \left(1 - \rho^2\right) T_{,z} = T_{,\rho\rho} + \rho^{-1} T_{,\rho} + T_{,zz} \] where dimensionless variables above are defined in terms of dimensional quantities pipe radius \(R\), temperature \(T_*\), upstream wall temperature \(T_1\), downstream wall temperature \(T_2\), thermal diffusivity \(\alpha\), and maximum centerline velocity \(U_{\text{max}}\) as follows:

\[ \rho = \frac{r}{R} \] \[ z = \frac{Z}{R} \] \[ T = \frac{T_*-T_1}{T_2-T_1} \] \[ \textbf{Pe} = R U_{\text{max}} / \alpha \]
